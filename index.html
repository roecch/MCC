Vaccine Stock Analysis, DS4200 F21

Haden Pelletier, Jacob Chung, Monroe Chung, Ally Chao, Varun Narahari, Stella Ikpatt

Course Project as part of DS4200 F21: Information Visualization, taught by Prof. Ab Mosca, Data Visualization @ Khoury, Northeastern University.

Abstract

Throughout the COVID pandemic, companies that developed vaccines saw a surge in their stock prices while many others suffered a decline. The purpose of this project is to analyze the relative price performance of six different vaccine/medical device companies. The primary objective is to utilize historical price data to uncover trends/correlations amidst the companies. We plan on accomplishing this by graphing the price data against other statistical measures such as volume (the number of shares traded during market hours).

Visualization explanation

The visualization on the left is a line chart, where each company corresponds to a line. In the legend, we have 6 companies all with a coresponding color. There is a filter where you can choose between open, close, high, low, adjusted close price and the line chart will update. When you hover over the line, the company name, date, and current price will show.

The visualization on the right is a bar chart. Each company has a bar corresponding to the total volume of stock. We used different color encodings to differentiate the bars. There is brushing and linking between the bar chart and the line chart. You can click on a bar, and the corresponding line will highlight in the line chart. If you select multiple bars, then multiple lines will be selected.
